Overview

Research

A single question runs through most of what I do: what does a delay change? Introducing a lag into an otherwise well-understood model can destabilise a steady state, create oscillations that were not there before, and shift the threshold between persistence and extinction.

Themes

Delay models in population biology and epidemiology

Maturation, latency, and incubation all enter naturally as delays. I study how they reshape thresholds such as the basic reproduction number $\mathcal{R}_0$, when they induce Hopf bifurcations, and what the resulting oscillations mean for control strategies. Stage structure, spatial patches, and harvesting sit inside this theme.

delay differential equations · Hopf bifurcation · basic reproduction number · stage structure

Within-host infection and immune response

Models of the interaction between a pathogen and the immune system, where the response to an antigen is not instantaneous. Distributed delays are more realistic here than discrete ones, and the analytical question is whether global stability results survive when the kernel is left general rather than fixed in advance.

distributed delay · global stability · Lyapunov functionals · immunology

Dynamics of coupled and neural systems

Networks of interacting units in which transmission takes time. Delay-coupled oscillators show synchrony, phase locking, and amplitude death depending on coupling strength and lag, and I am interested in how network structure interacts with those transitions.

delay coupling · synchronisation · neural networks · perturbation theory

Numerical and analytical methods

Alongside the modelling, work on schemes for equations with memory: sinc-collocation and iterative Laplace transform methods for time-fractional partial integro-differential equations with weakly singular kernels, together with the convergence analysis.

sinc-collocation · Laplace transform methods · fractional operators · convergence analysis
