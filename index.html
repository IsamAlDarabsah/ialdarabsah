About

I study systems whose present state depends on their whole history. Memory of that kind shows up in population dynamics, in tumour growth, and in wave propagation through non-homogeneous media, and the Caputo fractional derivative is a natural language for it.

My current work centres on fractional eco-epidemiological models with herd-behaviour predation, where the functional response $\Phi(X) = X^k$ with $0 < k < 1$ breaks the Lipschitz condition at the origin and forces a careful re-examination of well-posedness. In parallel I develop spectral and residual-power-series schemes for higher-order nonlinear PDEs of Kawahara type.

I teach calculus and mathematical methods in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at JUST, and I write in both Arabic and English.
